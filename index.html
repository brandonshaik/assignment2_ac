Who are the immigrants fighting for America?

By Brandon Shaik

Serving in the U.S. Military is often seen as the ultimate pledge to the country, sacrificing your life to fight for the rights and freedoms of the nation. But many of those who choose to serve this country arenâ€™t citizens at all.

A small but significant portion of foreign born military personnel who served in 2014 were not citizens, over 98,300 people. The military requires that any non-citizen who wishes to serve be a legal permanent resident, able to work in the U.S. However, many jobs in the military require citizenship for security clearances, leaving non-citizens with low-ranking jobs while they serve.

Bush signed an executive order allowing any member of the U.S. Military to apply for citizenship without the residency requirement.

The countries of origin for non-citizen military personnel varies widely, with the largest percentage coming from Mexico, followed by Canada and the Philippines.
